Books

These are in no particular order of preference.

  • The Mountains of My Life - Walter Bonatti The reflections of one of the world's seminal mountaineers.
  • Narcissus and Goldmund - Hermann Hesse Thanks to Tom McCarthy for the recommendation.
  • Butcher's Crossing - John Williams An overlooked classic in the Western genre.
  • True Grit - Charles Portis Read it in one sitting.
  • Border Trilogy - Cormac McCarthy My favourite trilogy, a glimpse into life at the frontier in a literal and emotional sense.
  • The Friendly Orange Glow - Brian Dear The essential telling of how cyberculture began.
  • Collected Fictions - Jorge Luis Borges The most important Spanish-language writer to be translated into English.
  • The Singapore Grip - J.G. Farrell
  • From Third World to First - Kuan Yew Lee
  • The General in His Labyrinth- Gabriel Garcia Marquez
  • Heat - Bill Buford An Amateur's Adventures as Kitchen Slave, Line Cook, Pasta-Maker, and Apprentice to a Dante-Quoting Butcher in Tuscany!
  • Scoop - Evelyn Waugh A timeless insight into journalism.
  • Shah of Shahs - Ryszard Kapuscinski Everything by Kapuscinski is brilliant but this is my favourite.
  • Ways of Seeing - John Berger The book that taught me how to appreciate art.
  • Tiepolo Pink -Roberto Calasso
  • Man's Search for Meaning - Viktor Frankl A book I return to.
  • The Tiger - John Vaillant A fascinating story about a hunt for a man-eating tiger in Siberia.
  • Cat's Cradle - Kurt Vonnegut
  • The Old Man and the Sea - Ernest Hemingway
  • Full Tilt - Dervla Murphy The book responsible for my love of travel writing.
  • The Old Patagonian Express - Paul Theroux
  • The Places in Between - Rory Stewart
  • The Lives of the Artists - Giorgio Vasari
  • Invisible Cities - Italo Calvino A masterfully written book that explains man's fascination with cities.
  • The Snow Leopard - Peter Matthiessen
  • The Hunters - James Salter
  • Operating Manual for Spaceship Earth - Buckminster Fuller
  • The Name of the Rose - Umberto Eco
